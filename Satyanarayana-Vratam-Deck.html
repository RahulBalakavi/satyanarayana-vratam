Preparation

What is the Satyanarayana Vratam

  • A worship of Lord Vishnu as Satyanarayana — the Lord who is Truth itself.
  • Performed on a Purnima (full moon), Ekadasi, Kartika/Vaishakha days, or any auspicious or happy occasion — a new home, a wedding, a wish fulfilled.
  • The heart of it is faith and the reading of the five-chapter Katha (story).
  • Open to all, easy to perform, asks for no fasting — only a pure heart and devotion.
Talk-track — The Satyanarayana Vratam is performed for every kind of auspicious beginning and thanksgiving — and simply to invite the Lord's grace. Lord Narayana Himself revealed it to sage Narada as the easiest path to happiness and liberation in the Kali age. Traditionally it is done in the evening, with family and friends, ending with the reading of the Katha and the sharing of prasadam.

Preparation

Setup — Mandapam, Kalasam, Prasadam

  • Clean the puja area; set up a mandapam with a picture or idol of Satyanarayana Swamy (Vishnu).
  • Arrange a Kalasam (water pot) with coconut and mango leaves.
  • Keep ready: tulasi, flowers, banana, panchamrutham (milk, curd, ghee, honey, sugar), haldi, kumkum, akshata, incense, lamp, betel.
  • Prepare the prasadam — the traditional 'sapada' offering: rava-kesari (sooji) of equal measures of rava, sugar, ghee and milk, with banana.
  • Invite family and friends — all who attend and take prasadam share the blessing.
Talk-track — The signature prasadam of this Vratam is the sapada-bhakshyam — 'sava-pavu', one-and-a-quarter measures each of rava, sugar, ghee and milk, made into kesari with banana. The Katha itself prescribes offering banana, ghee, sugar and wheat-flour. Keep tulasi ready, for the Lord loves it. A clean place invites a clean mind.

  • After the worship, read the five-chapter Katha — the heart of the Vratam.

The Katha

1 · The Lord Reveals the Vratam

  • At Naimisaranya, the sages ask Suta: in the Kali age, what easy vratam grants happiness and liberation?
  • Sage Narada, grieved at human suffering, goes to Vishnu and asks for a remedy.
  • Lord Narayana reveals the Satyanarayana Vratam — performed in the evening with family, offering banana, ghee, sugar and wheat-flour — and promises it fulfils every desire and grants liberation.
tap a line to jump
చాలా కాలం క్రితం, భూమిపై ప్రజల బాధలతో కృంగిపోయిన నారదుడు, భగవంతుడైన నారాయణ వద్దకు వెళ్లి వారి దుఃఖాన్ని తగ్గించే మార్గాన్ని అడిగాడు.
Long ago, the sage Narada, grieved by the suffering of people on earth, went to Lord Narayana and asked for a way to relieve their sorrow.
కాళి యుగానికి సులభమైన, శక్తివంతమైన ఆరాధన అయిన సత్యనారాయణ వ్రతాన్ని భగవంతుడు వెల్లడించాడు.
The Lord revealed the Satyanarayana Vratam, an easy and powerful worship for the age of Kali.
సాయంత్రం కుటుంబంతో, భక్తితో, సాధారణ ఆహారాన్ని సమర్పించి ఈ వ్రతాన్ని నిర్వహించే వ్యక్తి ప్రతి కష్టానికి నుండి విముక్తి పొందుతాడు.
Whoever performs this vratam in the evening with family and devotion, offering simple food, is freed from every difficulty.
అన్ని కోరికలు నెరవేరుతాయి, చివరికి భక్తుడు మోక్షాన్ని పొందుతాడు.
All desires are fulfilled, and in the end the devotee attains liberation.
॥ श्री सत्यनारायण स्वामिने नमः ॥
|| śrī satyanārāyaṇa svāmine namaḥ ||
॥ श्री सत्यनारायण स्वामिने नमः ॥

2 · The Poor Brahmin & the Woodcutter

  • A destitute Brahmin of Kashi; the Lord comes disguised as an old Brahmin and teaches him the Vratam.
  • He performs it, is freed of poverty, and keeps it every month.
  • A poor woodcutter sees him worshipping, learns the Vratam, vows and performs it — and he too prospers.

The Katha

3 · King Ulkamukha & the Merchant's Broken Vow

  • The righteous King Ulkamukha performs the Vratam on the riverbank.
  • A merchant, Sadhu Vanik, learns it and vows to perform it when blessed with a child; his wife Lilavati bears a daughter, Kalavati.
  • He keeps postponing the vow — and forgets it even at Kalavati's wedding. Displeased, the Lord lets misfortune fall: he and his son-in-law are falsely jailed for theft, and his wealth is lost.
tap a line to jump
ధర్మరాజు అయిన ఉల్కముఖుడు భద్రశిలా నది ఒడ్డున వ్రతం చేశాడు.
The righteous King Ulkamukha performed the vratam on the bank of the river Bhadrashila.
సాహు అనే వ్యాపారి చూస్తూ, వ్రతం నేర్చుకుని, బిడ్డ పుట్టగానే దానిని ఆచరించాలని ప్రతిజ్ఞ చేశాడు.
A merchant named Sadhu watched, learned the vratam, and vowed to perform it once he was blessed with a child.
కాలక్రమేణా అతని భార్య కాలావతి అనే కుమార్తెకు జన్మనిచ్చింది, కానీ ఆ వ్యాపారి తన ప్రతిజ్ఞను ఆలస్యం చేస్తూ, చివరికి ఆమె పెళ్లిలో కూడా దానిని మర్చిపోయాడు.
In time his wife bore a daughter, Kalavati, but the merchant kept delaying his vow, and at last forgot it even at her wedding.
అసంతృప్తి చెందిన లార్డ్ దురదృష్టాన్ని పంచాడు: వ్యాపారి మరియు అతని అల్లుడు దొంగతనం చేసినట్లు తప్పుడు ఆరోపణలు ఎదుర్కొని, ఖైదు చేయబడ్డారు, వారి సంపద పోయింది.
Displeased, the Lord let misfortune fall: the merchant and his son-in-law were falsely accused of theft and imprisoned, and their wealth was lost.

Talk-track — The third chapter is a warning against a forgotten vow. The merchant means well but keeps delaying, and at last forgets entirely — and the Lord, to remind him, withdraws His grace until the vow is honored.

The Katha

4 · The Boat of Leaves & the Forgotten Prasadam

  • Released after Lilavati and Kalavati resume the Vratam, the merchant sails home. The Lord, as a wandering monk, asks what is in his boat; he scoffs 'only leaves' — and his wealth turns to leaves.
  • He repents at the monk's feet, and the wealth is restored.
  • At home, Kalavati runs to her husband without taking the prasadam — and his boat sinks. A heavenly voice tells her to partake; she returns, takes the prasadam, and her husband reappears.

వ్యాపారి భార్య మరియు కుమార్తె మళ్ళీ వ్రతం ప్రారంభించినప్పుడు, ఆ ఇద్దరు వ్యక్తులను విడుదల చేసి ఇంటికి వెళ్ళడానికి నౌకలో ప్రయాణించారు.
When the merchant's wife and daughter resumed the vratam, the two men were released and set sail for home.
భగవంతుడు, ఒక సంచార సన్యాసి రూపంలో, వారి పడవలో ఏమి ఉందని అడిగాడు; వ్యాపారి అది పొడి ఆకులు మాత్రమే ఉన్నాయని తిట్టిపోయాడు.
The Lord, in the form of a wandering monk, asked what was in their boat; the merchant scornfully said it held only dry leaves.
అకస్మాత్తుగా సంపద ఆకులుగా మారి, వ్యాపారి ఆ సన్యాసి పాదాల వద్ద పడి, పశ్చాత్తాపపడ్డాడు, సంపద పునరుద్ధరించబడింది.
At once the wealth turned to leaves, until the merchant fell at the monk's feet and repented, and the riches were restored.
ఇంట్లో, అతని కుమార్తె కళావతి తన భర్తను ప్రణామము చేయుటకు ప్రసాదం తీసుకోకుండా పరుగెత్తి వెళ్లిపోయింది, అతని పడవ కనిపించకుండా మునిగిపోయింది; ఆమె తిరిగి వచ్చి ప్రసాదం తిన్నప్పుడు మాత్రమే అతను తిరిగి కనిపించాడు.
At home, his daughter Kalavati ran to greet her husband without taking the prasadam, and his boat sank from sight; only when she returned and ate the prasadam did he reappear.
॥ श्री सत्यनारायण स्वामिने नमः ॥
|| śrī satyanārāyaṇa svāmine namaḥ ||
॥ श्री सत्यनारायण स्वामिने नमः ॥
Talk-track — The fourth chapter teaches two lessons: never make light of the Lord's gifts, and never neglect the prasadam. The merchant's careless word turns gold to leaves; his daughter's skipped prasadam nearly costs her husband — both are set right the moment they bow and partake with faith.

The Katha

5 · King Tungadhwaja's Pride

  • King Tungadhwaja, out of pride, ignores a group of cowherds performing the Vratam and refuses their prasadam.
  • He loses his hundred sons and all his wealth and grain.
  • Realizing it is the Lord's displeasure, he returns, worships properly, and accepts the prasadam — and everything is restored. He too attains liberation.

రాజు తుంగధ్వజడు, గర్వంగా, నిర్లక్ష్యంగా, సత్యనారాయణ భగవానుడిని పూజించిన గొఱ్రెపాదులను చూసి, వారి ప్రసాదాన్ని స్వీకరించడానికి నిరాకరించాడు.
King Tungadhwaja, proud and unheeding, came upon cowherds worshipping Lord Satyanarayana and refused to accept their prasadam.
ఈ అగౌరవానికి అతను తన వంద మంది కుమారులను, తన సంపదను, ధాన్యాన్ని కోల్పోయాడు.
For this disrespect he lost his hundred sons and all his wealth and grain.
తన తప్పును గ్రహించిన అతను వినయంగా తిరిగి వచ్చి, భగవంతుడిని పూజించి, ప్రసాదాన్ని స్వీకరించి, ప్రతిదీ అతనికి తిరిగి ఇవ్వబడింది.
Realizing his fault, he returned humbly, worshipped the Lord, and accepted the prasadam, and everything was restored to him.
ఎవరైతే ఈ వ్రతాన్ని చేసి, భక్తితో ఈ పవిత్ర కథను వినగలరో వారు అన్ని బాధల నుండి విముక్తులై సత్య నివాసానికి చేరుకుంటారు.
Whoever performs this vratam and hears this holy story with devotion is freed from all sorrow and reaches the abode of truth.

Talk-track — The fifth chapter is the sharpest warning — against scorning the prasadam out of pride. King Tungadhwaja loses everything for it, and regains everything the moment he humbles himself and partakes. The phalashruti closes the Katha: whoever performs this rare vow and hears this holy story with devotion is freed from all sorrow and reaches the abode of Truth.

Conclusion

Kshamapana · Theertham · Udvasanam

  • Kshamapana — ask the Lord's forgiveness for any error or lack in the worship.
  • Take Theertham — the holy water; offer the final Maha Naivedyam.
  • Udvasanam — respectfully conclude, asking the Lord to remain gracious.
  • Distribute prasadam to everyone; share the sapada kesari and blessed tulasi.

Talk-track — We close by asking the Lord to forgive whatever was missing in mantra, ritual or devotion — He looks only at the heart. Take the theertham, offer the final naivedyam, and conclude the worship. Then distribute the prasadam — the sapada kesari and blessed tulasi — to everyone present, and carry the Lord's grace with you. Satyanarayana Swamy ki Jai!
